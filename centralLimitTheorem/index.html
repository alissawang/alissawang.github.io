The Central Limit Theorem

The Central Limit Theorem states that if we repeatedly take random samples from a distribution of any shape, the means of each of those samples will themselves fall under a normal distribution.

Note that the following conditions must be true:

  1. The samples are independent and identically distributed, i.e. the outcome of one sample should not affect another.
  2. The population distribution has finite variance.
  3. The samples are sufficiently large (for most distributions, we need n >= 30). (We'll prove this on the next page).
