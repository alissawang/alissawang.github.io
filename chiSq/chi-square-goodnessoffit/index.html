What is the likelihood of getting this chi-squared value if our null hypothesis is true?

First, we need to find the right probability distribution curve to tell us the probability of seeing our chi-squared value.

We choose a probability distribution based on the degrees of freedom in our data, i.e. the number of independent pieces of information we had to work with.

So let's find the degrees of freedom in our observed samples.
