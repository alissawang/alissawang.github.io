Statistical Power

Statistical power is the likelihood of correctly detecting an existing effect.

Take, for example, a simple z-test for means.

If we take a sample and the mean is within the shaded area under the curve where p<0, then we'll reject the null hypothesis h0 that the true mean is 0.

But let's say we have an alternate hypothesis (h1) that the true mean is 0.3. If that's true, what is the probability that we'll correctly reject the null hypothesis?

In a world where h1 is true and our alpha value is 0.05, we'll correctly reject the null 99% of the time. This is the power of our test.
