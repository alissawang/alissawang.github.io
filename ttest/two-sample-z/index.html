Two-Sample Z-Test

Use cases: Two separately drawn samples each with N≥30

The Kansas Farm Bureau claims that cows produce an average of 6.3 gallons of milk every day. This seems like a lot to you, so you find a random sample of 30 cows and measure how many gallons of milk they each produce in one day:

Our sample of cows produced 7 gallons of milk on average. This is higher than 6.3 gallons as claimed by the Kansas Farm Bureau.

If the Farm Bureau is right, what is the probability of seeing our sample average of 7 gallons just by chance?

Given our null hypothesis h0 that the true average is 6.3 gallons, we'll need to calculate the probability of getting a sample value as far away from 6.3 as we saw.
